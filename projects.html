These are projects I have designed, built, and shipped for clients I care about. Each one reflects the same core commitment: semantic structure, accessible markup, clean code, and results that hold up in the real world.

Client Projects

Kentucky Pikes, Pi Kappa Alpha at the University of Kentucky

The official chapter website for Pi Kappa Alpha at the University of Kentucky. Built to serve a real organization with real needs: recruitment information, chapter history, member resources, and event coverage. The site is fast, credible, and easy to navigate because for prospective members, a website is often the first impression.

Structured data on the site helps the chapter surface in search results when students are looking for Greek life options at UK, which directly supports their recruitment goals.

  • HTML5
  • CSS3 / Flexbox
  • JavaScript
  • JSON-LD
  • WCAG Accessible
Visit kentuckypikes.com

Outdoor Bills, Bill Leroy of the Savannah Bananas

A personal website built for Bill Leroy, baseball player for the Savannah Bananas. The project was a close collaboration from start to finish. Bill had a clear sense of his identity, and my job was to translate that into a web experience that feels as distinct as his personality.

Built on a fully semantic HTML5 structure with Flexbox layout, validated markup, and JSON-LD structured data. Fast, mobile friendly, and accessible so fans can reach it on any device.

  • HTML5
  • CSS3 / Flexbox
  • JavaScript
  • JSON-LD
  • Mobile First
Visit outdoorbills.co
